# Sunnybrook Early Learning — Family Handbook

Welcome to Sunnybrook. This handbook describes how we care for, teach, and partner with the families in our program. Policies follow New Mexico CYFD licensing standards and USDA CACFP nutrition guidelines, and are adapted from the City of Albuquerque Division of Child & Family Development's Family Handbook.

If something in this handbook is unclear, please ask your child's Head Teacher or email the director at `hello@sunnybrook.example`.

## Philosophy

We believe that working with young children is a privilege. Our classrooms are designed to be safe, warm, and purposefully busy — with the same small details you would want in your own home. Teachers plan the day around children's interests, emerging skills, and the rhythm of the group. We use the **Nurtured Heart Approach**: we notice and describe a child's positive actions every day, because children build self-control when their attention and energy is given to what is going *right*, not what is going wrong.

Our doors are always open to hear about your child's successes, needs, and concerns.

## Hours of Operation

Sunnybrook is open **Monday through Friday, 7:30 a.m. – 5:30 p.m.** We are closed on weekends.

- **Infants (6 weeks – 18 months):** full day 7:30–5:30
- **Toddlers (18 months – 3 years):** full day 7:30–5:30
- **Preschool (3–5 years):** full day 7:30–5:30, with a Pre-K core day 8:30–2:30 inside it

Families must schedule arrivals before **9:30 a.m.** so that teachers can plan the day's activities around the full group. If you will arrive after 9:30, please call the center so we can reserve lunch and an afternoon rest spot for your child.

## Weather & Emergency Closures

Sunnybrook generally follows **Albuquerque Public Schools** for snow, ice, and severe-weather closures. When APS closes or has a delayed start, Sunnybrook closes or delays by the same amount.

We announce closures by **8:00 p.m. the night before** when possible, or by **6:00 a.m. the day of** for overnight weather events. Notifications go out by:

1. Text message to the enrolling parent's phone on file
2. Email
3. A banner on the Sunnybrook website

If the center must close mid-day because of an emergency (power outage, water main, severe weather moving in), we will call every family on the pick-up list and ask them to pick up as soon as possible. Please keep your emergency contacts current.

## Enrollment & Tours

We welcome tours by appointment, Tuesday through Thursday, 9:30–11:00 a.m. or 3:00–4:30 p.m. Tours take about 40 minutes and include a walk through the classrooms, a chance to meet the director, and time for your questions.

To request a tour, message us through the chat on sunnybrook.example or call (505) 555-0142.

When you're ready to enroll, you'll complete:

1. **Enrollment application** — your child's information, emergency contacts, pick-up list (at least two local contacts)
2. **Immunization record** (see § Immunizations) or an exemption certificate
3. **Most recent well-child visit** documentation
4. **Nutrition / Allergy form** if your child has any food allergy, signed by a physician
5. **Registration fee and first month's tuition**

Spots are not held without a signed enrollment agreement and payment.

## Arrival, Departure & Pick-Up

Children must be **accompanied to and from the classroom by an adult** (18 or older). Please sign your child in and out on the tablet at the classroom door every day, including partial-day appointments.

**Pick-up list.** Only people on your written pick-up list may pick up your child. Anyone not on the list — including someone the enrolling parent sends last-minute — must be added in writing first and must present photo ID at pick-up. We check ID every time, even for people we recognize.

**Late pick-up.** If you will be later than 5:30 p.m., call the center. After the center closes we will begin calling emergency contacts. If no one has reached us 30 minutes after closing, we contact Albuquerque Police; please do everything you can to avoid this.

**Impairment at pick-up.** For your child's safety, if the adult arriving to pick up appears to be under the influence of alcohol or other substances, we will ask you to arrange for a different adult on the pick-up list to come. If we cannot reach one, we are required to contact APD and Child Protective Services.

## Illness Policy

A sick child stays home. If your child is not well enough to fully participate in class activities, including outdoor play, please keep them home. Our policy follows New Mexico CYFD licensing; your child must stay home if any of the following is true:

- **Fever of 100.4°F or higher** within the past **24 hours** (fever-free for 24 hours *without* fever-reducing medication before returning)
- Vomiting or diarrhea within the past **24 hours**
- Pink eye or symptoms that could be pink eye (redness, swelling, discharge)
- Cloudy (not clear) runny nose
- Continuous coughing, especially during rest
- On antibiotics for less than **24 hours**
- Undiagnosed rash
- Any symptom of a contagious illness (chicken pox, strep, hand-foot-mouth, ringworm, crusted or oozing lesions, etc.)

If your child develops any of the above at the center, we will call you to pick up. **Children must be picked up within one hour of our call.** Teachers may err on the side of caution and call even when you believe symptoms are mild — the goal is to protect your child, the other children, and staff.

After a fever, vomiting, or diarrhea, your child may return once they have been symptom-free for 24 hours without medication. For contagious illnesses (pink eye, strep, hand-foot-mouth, chicken pox, etc.), we may ask for a **note from your pediatrician** clearing return.

If your child is hospitalized or seen in an ER, we need a medical release from the treating physician before they return.

## Medication

We administer medication only with a signed **Medication Authorization Form**, one per medication. The form records the child's name, the medication, dose, and times. This applies to over-the-counter items too — including sunscreen, diaper cream, lotion, lip balm, teething gel, and children's Tylenol.

Requirements:

- Medication must be in the **original container**, labeled with your child's name
- Give medication directly to a teacher — **never** leave it in a cubby, backpack, or lunch bag
- Prescription medication must be current (we return expired medication)
- **No cough drops** at any age (choking hazard)

A teacher records every dose administered — child, medication, time, dose, initials — and the record is kept for six months. Parents initial the daily log at pick-up.

If a medication requires specialized training (rescue inhalers, EpiPen, seizure medication, etc.), the parent is responsible for arranging that training with staff before the medication is administered at the center.

## Immunizations

New Mexico state regulations require a current immunization record on file for every enrolled child. Children **cannot attend** without either an up-to-date record or a current exemption certificate (medical or religious, per NMSA 24-5-3).

If your child receives a vaccine during the year, please bring a copy of the updated record to the office within two weeks so we can update the file. CYFD conducts random spot-checks of child files.

If a vaccine-preventable illness is circulating at the center, under-vaccinated children may need to stay home until it has cleared, for their safety and the safety of other children.

## Meals & Food Allergies

Sunnybrook participates in the **USDA Child and Adult Care Food Program (CACFP)**. Meals meet CACFP nutrition standards, and the same meals are offered to every child. Our meal schedule:

- **Breakfast** — 8:00 a.m.
- **Morning snack** — 9:45 a.m.
- **Lunch** — 11:30 a.m.
- **Afternoon snack** — 2:45 p.m.

**Outside food** is generally not permitted. Exceptions — birthdays, classroom potlucks, cultural celebrations — must be arranged in advance with the Head Teacher and must be **store-bought with a visible nutrition label**, peanut-free, and labeled with the child's name and the date.

**Food allergies.** If your child has a food allergy, a physician-signed **Nutrition / Allergy Form** is required before the first day. We share it with the kitchen so meals can be adjusted. We accommodate medically documented allergies; we do our best with religious and cultural restrictions; we do not accommodate lifestyle dietary preferences.

**Breastfeeding infants.** Bring expressed breast milk in bottles labeled with your child's name and the date pumped. We also keep iron-fortified formula on hand.

## Curriculum & Daily Rhythm

Our curriculum is play-based and built around the **NM Early Learning Guidelines** and the Nurtured Heart Approach. Preschool classrooms run on a themed monthly focus; teachers plan activities across literacy, math, science, art, music, and large-motor play that tie back to the theme.

A typical preschool day:

| Time | Activity |
|---|---|
| 7:30 – 8:30 | Arrival, free choice, quiet books |
| 8:30 – 9:00 | Breakfast, hand-washing |
| 9:00 – 9:45 | Morning circle (weather, calendar, theme intro) |
| 9:45 – 10:45 | Learning centers (dramatic play, blocks, manipulatives, art) |
| 10:45 – 11:30 | Outdoor play |
| 11:30 – 12:15 | Lunch |
| 12:15 – 2:30 | Rest time (full nap for younger children, quiet activity for older) |
| 2:30 – 3:15 | Afternoon snack, story, small-group work |
| 3:15 – 4:30 | Outdoor play or open centers |
| 4:30 – 5:30 | Free choice, clean-up, departure |

Toddler and infant rooms follow the child's own rhythm for feeding, sleep, and diapering, with shared times for outdoor play and music.

We do a formal **developmental screening** twice a year (fall and spring) using the Ages & Stages Questionnaire. Parents get a conference with the Head Teacher after each screening.

## Communication with Families

- **Daily sheets** — infants and toddlers go home with a daily sheet covering feeds, naps, diapers, and notes
- **Weekly classroom email** — photos (only of your own child on request) and a recap of what the class has been exploring
- **Monthly newsletter** — menus, upcoming closures, curriculum theme, and the director's note
- **Conferences** — twice a year, or by request any time
- **Parent bulletin board** — just inside the front door; reportable diseases, menu, and weekly schedule are posted here
- **Open-door policy** — families are welcome to visit the classroom any time. Please check in at the front desk first so we can let the teacher know.

If you need to reach your child's teacher during the day, please call the front desk rather than texting directly — teachers do not have phones in the classroom so that their attention stays with the children.

## Sunnybrook Traditions

A few things that make Sunnybrook feel like Sunnybrook:

**Muffin Mornings.** The first Friday of every month we open at 7:45 with fresh muffins and coffee on the front porch. Parents who can linger are welcome to — it's the easiest way to meet the other families in your child's classroom.

**Garden Harvest.** Our back courtyard has four raised beds that the preschoolers plant in March, tend through summer, and harvest in September. Whatever they pick becomes part of the afternoon snack that day. Tomatoes are always the highlight; the great green bean glut of 2024 is still spoken of.

**Butterfly Release.** Every June, the outgoing Pre-K class raises painted-lady caterpillars in the classroom. On their last day before kindergarten, the whole center gathers in the back courtyard to release the butterflies together. Siblings, grandparents, and alumni are invited. It takes about 20 minutes and usually a few tissues.
